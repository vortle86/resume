What's your name again?

My name is Elisheba, pronounced ellie-sheva. The b sounds like a v, and it helps many people to imagine pronouncing two words.

I'll never get that!

Sure you will. Here's what it sounds like, so now you can practice 100 times each night before you go to bed!

That's still too hard.

Can I just call you Ellie, Elizabeth, Alyssa?

Seriously though, I realize my name is tough, but I appreciate any attempt- don't worry about trying to say it perfectly.

Where did your name come from?

Elisheba is Hebrew, an earlier form of the name that later became Elizabeth. It's rare here, but in Israel and in some American Orthodox Jewish communities, it's a very common name.

Where have you been all my life?

I'm a Colorado Native and grew up in Denver. I received my BA from St John's College in Santa Fe and have spent most of my career working in human services (supporting at-risk youth and people with disabilities) and education (as a special education teacher from kinder to postsecondary.) I've traveled throughout Latin America and visited Israel, but I keep ending up back home in Colorado.

Why coding?

My experience, while technology rich, has not been primarily tech-focused. Both my father and brother are developers, and as a result I grew up with lots of exposure to and experiences with computers and software including some beginning programming experience. I learned to be curious and to try to solve my own problems, as well as to use available resources (human and other) to figure things out. As a result of this active outlook toward technology and software, I have been able to develop a broader-than-average skill set with basic office and personal applications which has made me a valuable troubleshooting resource among my colleagues throughout my career.

I also have extensive experience in studying languages (Spanish, French, Ancient Greek, and Hebrew), and while I understand the serious distinctions between programming languages and communication languages, I find that intimate understanding with the structure and function of linguistic elements and an ability to combine, parse, and translate between those elements is supportive when working with code.

Possibly my most robust skillset when considering studying software development is my mathematical knowledge. I have studied and taught advanced mathematics throughout my educational and professional career including graduate work in mathematics education. I thrive on finding patterns, solving problems, and working within structure.

I'm excited to begin my new career as a developer in a setting which allows me use my energy and experience while developing my technical skills and serving as part of a team.

What else floats your boat?

Somehow, even when I'm not practicing and studying to become a Level 49 CodeWizard, I manage to keep busy.

I'm interested in xeriscape gardening and have been slowly working to landscape my property in ways that are respectful of our state's limited water resources and ecological balance.

I'm also a former professional dog trainer. These days I only train my own dogs, but we have plenty to work on! My current dogs, Inspector SpaceTime (Spector for short) and Katniss, are both young and inexperienced, but I hope to compete with them in dog sports including obedience, rally obedience, and agility. We also play with herding, nosework, and lurecoursing just for fun.

In order to maintain my license as a Coloradan, I'm legally obligated to mention that I love to get outdoors, hike, camp, and explore in our beautiful state! (Fortunately enough, it's also true!)
